How To Read It

  • Rising breakevens usually mean inflation expectations are firming, while falling breakevens usually point to softer nominal-growth expectations.
  • This chart is most useful when yields are moving because it helps tell you whether the move is about inflation, growth, or both.
  • Pair it with the 10-year yield and the yield curve because rates matter more once you know what kind of macro message they are sending.
