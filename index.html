Participation has thinned out quickly. Only 18.3% of S&P 500 members are above the 20DMA and 19.7% are above the 50DMA, while 44.2% remain above the 200DMA, so the near-term tape is softer than the longer-term damage count suggests.

Energy (+10.9%), Utilities (-3.6%), and Basic Materials (-3.7%) are leading the 30-day sector board. Energy plus defensives is usually a sign that investors still want exposure, but with a hedge against softer growth or stickier inflation.

Options are still carrying a meaningful fear premium: VIX sits at 30.6 versus 14.6 realized volatility. At the same time the 10Y minus 2Y curve is +0.53%, which is materially cleaner than inversion. Net: the macro backdrop is less hostile, but the internal tape still deserves respect.
